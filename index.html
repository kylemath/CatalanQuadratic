The Catalan Series Solution

To see how the Catalan numbers relate to quadratic equations, consider

\[1 - \alpha + t\alpha^2 = 0 \quad (2)\]

which according to the quadratic formula has the solution in radicals:

\[\alpha = \frac{1}{2t}(1 \pm \sqrt{1-4t}).\]

Applying Newton's binomial expansion to the minus sign solution reveals the generating series of the Catalan numbers as the formal power series solution:

\[\alpha = \sum_{n\geq 0}\frac{(2n)!}{n!(n+1)!}t^n = \sum_{n\geq 0}C_n t^n.\]

The result justifies the lovely identity:

\[(1 + t + 2t^2 + 5t^3 + 14t^4 + 42t^5 + \cdots)^2 = 1 + 2t + 5t^2 + 14t^3 + 42t^4 + \cdots.\]
